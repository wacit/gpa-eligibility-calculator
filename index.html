Methodology & disclaimers

Overall GPA: percentage → letter via the chosen scale → points (A=4…F=0). Weighted adds the Honors/AP bonus to passing grades, then averages by credit hours.

NCAA core GPA: only core-academic courses count — English, Math (Algebra I+), Natural/Physical Science, Social Science, Foreign Language, and Other Academic. Electives such as Band, PE, Health, Personal Finance, and study halls are excluded. No honors/AP weighting and no +/-. Each core semester course = 0.5 unit. A failing grade earns 0 units (consistent with the NCAA “best 16 core courses” rule). DI full qualifier ≥ 2.3, DII ≥ 2.2. 16 core units are required by graduation (10, incl. 7 in Eng/Math/Sci, before the 7th semester).

NAIA: uses the student’s overall (cumulative) GPA on a 4.0 scale. A 2.3 overall GPA satisfies one of NAIA’s three requirements; a student must meet at least two of: GPA ≥ 2.3, ACT 18/SAT 970, or top-50% class rank.

Verify with officials. Confirm your school’s exact grading scale and weighting, and check the school’s official NCAA-approved core-course list at the NCAA Eligibility Center. This tool is for planning only and does not determine eligibility.
