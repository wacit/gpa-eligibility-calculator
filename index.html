How to use this calculator

Estimate a student's GPA on both grading scales and check it against college-athletics academic standards. Everything runs in your browser — nothing you type is uploaded or saved anywhere but this device.

1 · Enter the student's courses

List one row per semester grade (the grades that earn credit). Three ways to add them:

  • Type them in — click + Add course and fill each row.
  • Import a spreadsheet — click Download CSV template, fill it in Excel/Google Sheets, then Import CSV…. Not sure where your grades are? Click 📥 Where's my data? for how to get them out of PowerSchool, Infinite Campus, Skyward, and more.
  • Paste, or convert a photo with AI — click 📋 Paste / AI import. Paste rows from a spreadsheet/portal, or copy the built-in AI prompt, give it a photo of a transcript in ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini, and paste the result back.
  • Try it first — click Load sample data to see a worked example, then Clear all when ready.

Save or move your work: 💾 Save profile downloads everything (courses, scores, settings) as a file; 📂 Load profile restores it on any device; 🔗 Copy share link makes a link you can send to a counselor or open elsewhere (the data rides in the link — still nothing sent to a server).

What each column means:

  • Term — e.g. Freshman S1. Just a label.
  • Course — the class name.
  • Category — pick the subject. Only academic categories (English, Math, Science, Social Science, Foreign Language, Other Academic) count toward the NCAA core GPA. Choose Elective / Non-Core for things like Band, PE, Health, or study hall.
  • % — the percentage grade (e.g. 88). The letter grade is figured automatically.
  • Credit hrs — used for the overall GPA. Enter 0 for a failed/no-credit class if your school gives no credit for it.
  • Track — Regular, Honors, or AP. Only affects the weighted overall GPA (NCAA/NAIA ignore weighting).
  • Plan? — check this for future/senior-year courses you're projecting (see step 5).

2 · Match your school's grading scale

Open Grading scale settings and set the cutoffs to match your school exactly. Defaults are the common 7-point (A≥93) and 10-point (A≥90) scales. You can also set the Honors/AP bonus and the NCAA/NAIA pass thresholds here.

3 · Add test scores & class rank (optional)

In the Test scores & class rank card, enter the student's ACT composite and/or SAT total, and optionally the class rank (top __% of class). These feed the NAIA "2 of 3" check below. Have only one test? The app shows a SAT↔ACT concordance estimate with a one-click "use it." Note: the NCAA dropped ACT/SAT from DI/DII initial eligibility (permanent since 2023), so scores don't change the NCAA result.

4 · Read the results

  • Overall GPA — your full GPA on both scales, unweighted and weighted. Green = the 10-point (new) result.
  • NCAA Core GPA — uses core-academic courses only, no weighting. The badges show pass/fail vs DI ≥2.3 and DII ≥2.2, and it tracks progress toward the 16 required core units.
  • NAIA Eligibility — checks the "2 of 3" rule: overall GPA ≥ 2.3, test leg (ACT ≥ 18 / SAT ≥ 970), or top-50% class rank. If two are met it shows QUALIFIES; if only the GPA or test leg is met it shows it still needs the top-50% rank leg.

5 · Project the senior year (optional)

Add upcoming courses with the Plan? box checked (or use + Add planned (senior) course). Then use the View toggle above the results to switch between Completed only and Projected (incl. planned) to see where the GPA is headed at graduation.

6 · Explore colleges (optional)

In the College search & match card, search by school name, state, and/or major, and narrow by NCAA/NAIA division (D-I / D-II / D-III / NAIA) and by a men's or women's sport the school sponsors (e.g. only NAIA schools in Ohio with men's soccer). Each result is labeled with its division and shows cost (net price & tuition), admission rate, and the school's SAT/ACT middle-50% ranges, with a link to the school. Star (☆) schools to add them to Compare — a side-by-side table of cost, admission rate, division, and your match. Use the Show 25 / 50 / 100 selector to see more results. Note: filters combine — a school must match all of them, so to find one specific school type its name and leave the others on "Any"; pick a state first when browsing by division/sport. If you've entered ACT/SAT above, you'll also get an academic match badge — Likely Target Reach — based on how your scores compare to that school's ranges and selectivity. It's an estimate, not a guarantee (recruited athletes may get extra admissions support). Data is the federal College Scorecard; only your search terms are sent — your scores stay in your browser.

7 · Save or share

  • Export results (CSV) — the GPA/eligibility summary.
  • Export courses (CSV) — your course list to reopen later.
  • Print / Save as PDF — a clean printout to share with a counselor or coach.

Good to know

  • Your data is remembered only in this browser on this device — close and reopen and it's still here; open it elsewhere and you start fresh.
  • This tool is for planning only. Confirm your school's exact scale/weighting and its official NCAA-approved core-course list at the NCAA Eligibility Center; official eligibility is determined by the NCAA/NAIA.

Getting your grades out of your school's system

Most parent/student portals don't have a one-click export, so the fastest reliable source is usually your transcript. Below are the common platforms and where the grades live. Then put one row per semester course into the CSV template (or type them in).

⭐ Best source: your transcript

Ask your school counselor or registrar for an unofficial transcript (almost always a free PDF, often same-day). It lists every course, final grade, and credit for all years — exactly what this tool needs. Many schools also post it in Naviance or SCOIR. This beats screen-scraping the portal term by term.

PowerSchool (parent / student portal)

  • Sign in to your district's portal (usually yourdistrict.powerschool.com or the PowerSchool Mobile app).
  • Click Grades and Attendance for the current term, and Grade History in the left menu for past semesters — Grade History is the best in-portal view of final grades.
  • Click a grade to see the percentage/details if your school enables it.
  • No CSV export in most parent portals — use the on-screen grades to fill the template, or press Ctrl/Cmd + P → Save as PDF to keep a copy.

Infinite Campus (Campus Parent / Student)

  • Open Grades; use the term selector to view past semesters.
  • Check Documents or Reports for report cards / transcript PDFs.

Skyward (Family / Student Access)

  • Gradebook shows current grades; Portfolio holds report cards and the transcript (PDF).

Canvas · Schoology · Google Classroom (LMS)

  • These show current course grades only, not full history — open Grades within each course for the live percentage. Use your transcript for past years.

Then: fill the template

  • Click Download CSV template and add one row per semester course: Term, Course, Category, Percent, CreditHours, Track, Planned, then Import CSV…. Or just + Add course and type them.
  • Only have letter grades? This tool uses percentages. Enter a number inside that letter's band (e.g. A→95, B→85, C→75), or set your school's exact cutoffs in Grading scale settings and use a representative %.
  • Mark Category correctly so NCAA core courses are counted (electives like Band/PE = "Elective / Non-Core").

Tip: if a counselor sends a transcript PDF, you can read off each semester's courses and final grades straight into the template — it's the quickest path for all four years.

Paste grades or use an AI

Option A — turn a photo or PDF into CSV with an AI

The fastest way to handle a printed transcript or report card. Takes about 2 minutes end-to-end. Free options: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot — any chatbot that accepts file attachments.

1. Get the transcript in digital form

  • Paper copy? Use your phone camera or scanner. One clear photo per page is fine. Make sure all grades are readable — no glare, no fingers covering text.
  • PDF from the school? Save it from your email or portal (the unofficial transcript PDF works great).
  • Screenshots? Take one per term/year from the PowerSchool, Infinite Campus, or Skyward portal.

2. Open an AI chatbot that accepts files

  • ChatGPT (chat.openai.com) — free; click the 📎 paperclip on the message box to attach.
  • Claude (claude.ai) — free; drag & drop the file into the chat, or click the 📎 attach button.
  • Google Gemini (gemini.google.com) — free; click the + plus icon next to the message box.
  • Microsoft Copilot (copilot.microsoft.com) — free; click the 📎 paperclip icon.

3. Attach the transcript AND paste the prompt

In the same message:

  • Click the attach button and pick your transcript file (PDF, JPG, PNG, or HEIC all work).
  • Click 🤖 Copy AI prompt above (it copies an exact, foolproof prompt that tells the AI what columns to use, how to categorize, and how to convert letter grades).
  • Paste the prompt into the message box (Ctrl+V or Cmd+V).
  • Press send. Wait 5–15 seconds.

4. Get the CSV back from the AI

The AI will respond with rows that look like:

Term,Course,Category,Percent,CreditHours,Track,Planned
Freshman S1,Algebra I,Math,86,1,Regular,No
Freshman S1,English 9,English,88,1,Honors,No
Freshman S1,Band,Elective / Non-Core,95,1,Regular,No

You have two ways to bring it in:

  • Paste it back here — copy the CSV (triple-click or drag to select, then Ctrl/Cmd+C), paste into the box below in Option B, and click Add to my courses. You can also click 💾 Save as CSV file to keep a copy on your computer.
  • Download a .csv file directly from the AI — ChatGPT will often offer a downloadable my_grades.csv in its response (a small file/download icon). Claude shows it as a CSV "artifact" you can download. Once you have the file, use Import CSV… in the calculator instead.

5. Spot-check the result

  • Number of rows looks right? If you had 24 semester grades on the transcript, you should see ~24 rows.
  • Category column accurate? Especially watch Band, PE, Health, Personal Finance — they must be "Elective / Non-Core" or they'll incorrectly count toward NCAA core GPA.
  • Letter grades only? The prompt tells the AI to use A=95, B=85, C=75, D=65, F=55. If your school uses different cutoffs, adjust Grading scale settings in section 2.
  • Something missing or wrong? Just ask the AI in a follow-up message: "You skipped sophomore year — please add those courses too" or "Reclassify PE as Elective".

Tips for blurry photos or weird PDFs

  • If the AI says "I can't read this clearly" — take a brighter, straight-on photo or zoom in to retake.
  • For multi-page transcripts, you can attach multiple images in one message (most chatbots accept up to 10).
  • If only letter grades are shown and you want precision, retake the photo of the legend/scale (e.g., "A = 94–100, B = 87–93") and the AI will use the midpoint.
  • Privacy note: when you send a file to an AI, that data leaves your device. Most free chatbots use it for training unless you turn off "improve the model" or use a temporary/incognito chat.

Option B — paste rows directly

Paste from the AI, a spreadsheet, or a school portal — one course per line. Columns can be separated by tabs, commas, or spaces. Best order: Term, Course, Category, Percent, CreditHours, Track. At minimum each line needs a course name and a 0–100 percent.

After importing, please review the Category column — it drives the NCAA core-GPA, and auto-detected categories aren't always perfect.

Methodology & disclaimers

Overall GPA: percentage → letter via the chosen scale → points (A=4…F=0). Weighted adds the Honors/AP bonus to passing grades, then averages by credit hours.

NCAA core GPA: only core-academic courses count — English, Math (Algebra I+), Natural/Physical Science, Social Science, Foreign Language, and Other Academic. Electives such as Band, PE, Health, Personal Finance, and study halls are excluded. No honors/AP weighting and no +/-. Each core semester course = 0.5 unit. A failing grade earns 0 units (consistent with the NCAA “best 16 core courses” rule). DI full qualifier ≥ 2.3, DII ≥ 2.2. 16 core units are required by graduation (10, incl. 7 in Eng/Math/Sci, before the 7th semester).

NAIA: uses the student’s overall (cumulative) GPA on a 4.0 scale. A 2.3 overall GPA satisfies one of NAIA’s three requirements; a student must meet at least two of: GPA ≥ 2.3, ACT 18/SAT 970, or top-50% class rank.

Verify with officials. Confirm your school’s exact grading scale and weighting, and check the school’s official NCAA-approved core-course list at the NCAA Eligibility Center. This tool is for planning only and does not determine eligibility.
