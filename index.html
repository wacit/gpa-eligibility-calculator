How to use this calculator

Estimate a student's GPA on both grading scales and check it against college-athletics academic standards. Everything runs in your browser — nothing you type is uploaded or saved anywhere but this device.

1 · Enter the student's courses

List one row per semester grade (the grades that earn credit). Three ways to add them:

  • Type them in — click + Add course and fill each row.
  • Import a spreadsheet — click Download CSV template, fill it in Excel/Google Sheets, then Import CSV….
  • Try it first — click Load sample data to see a worked example, then Clear all when ready.

What each column means:

  • Term — e.g. Freshman S1. Just a label.
  • Course — the class name.
  • Category — pick the subject. Only academic categories (English, Math, Science, Social Science, Foreign Language, Other Academic) count toward the NCAA core GPA. Choose Elective / Non-Core for things like Band, PE, Health, or study hall.
  • % — the percentage grade (e.g. 88). The letter grade is figured automatically.
  • Credit hrs — used for the overall GPA. Enter 0 for a failed/no-credit class if your school gives no credit for it.
  • Track — Regular, Honors, or AP. Only affects the weighted overall GPA (NCAA/NAIA ignore weighting).
  • Plan? — check this for future/senior-year courses you're projecting (see step 4).

2 · Match your school's grading scale

Open Grading scale settings and set the cutoffs to match your school exactly. Defaults are the common 7-point (A≥93) and 10-point (A≥90) scales. You can also set the Honors/AP bonus and the NCAA/NAIA pass thresholds here.

3 · Read the results

  • Overall GPA — your full GPA on both scales, unweighted and weighted. Green = the 10-point (new) result.
  • NCAA Core GPA — uses core-academic courses only, no weighting. The badges show pass/fail vs DI ≥2.3 and DII ≥2.2, and it tracks progress toward the 16 required core units.
  • NAIA GPA — your overall GPA vs the 2.3 benchmark (one of NAIA's "2 of 3" requirements).

4 · Project the senior year (optional)

Add upcoming courses with the Plan? box checked (or use + Add planned (senior) course). Then use the View toggle above the results to switch between Completed only and Projected (incl. planned) to see where the GPA is headed at graduation.

5 · Save or share

  • Export results (CSV) — the GPA/eligibility summary.
  • Export courses (CSV) — your course list to reopen later.
  • Print / Save as PDF — a clean printout to share with a counselor or coach.

Good to know

  • Your data is remembered only in this browser on this device — close and reopen and it's still here; open it elsewhere and you start fresh.
  • This tool is for planning only. Confirm your school's exact scale/weighting and its official NCAA-approved core-course list at the NCAA Eligibility Center; official eligibility is determined by the NCAA/NAIA.

Methodology & disclaimers

Overall GPA: percentage → letter via the chosen scale → points (A=4…F=0). Weighted adds the Honors/AP bonus to passing grades, then averages by credit hours.

NCAA core GPA: only core-academic courses count — English, Math (Algebra I+), Natural/Physical Science, Social Science, Foreign Language, and Other Academic. Electives such as Band, PE, Health, Personal Finance, and study halls are excluded. No honors/AP weighting and no +/-. Each core semester course = 0.5 unit. A failing grade earns 0 units (consistent with the NCAA “best 16 core courses” rule). DI full qualifier ≥ 2.3, DII ≥ 2.2. 16 core units are required by graduation (10, incl. 7 in Eng/Math/Sci, before the 7th semester).

NAIA: uses the student’s overall (cumulative) GPA on a 4.0 scale. A 2.3 overall GPA satisfies one of NAIA’s three requirements; a student must meet at least two of: GPA ≥ 2.3, ACT 18/SAT 970, or top-50% class rank.

Verify with officials. Confirm your school’s exact grading scale and weighting, and check the school’s official NCAA-approved core-course list at the NCAA Eligibility Center. This tool is for planning only and does not determine eligibility.
